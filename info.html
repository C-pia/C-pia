About C-pia! Magazine


Run, produced, and mainly written by one person, C-pia! Magazine is best described as a fancy personal blog by a fancy personal writer.
Styled after old school gaming and anime magazines, it serves as a retro-focused spinoff of the modern gaming and anime blog plsnohate.wordpress.com.

C-pia! incorporates the three main foci of plsnohate: Reviews, analysis and modding.
Dyed by over 15 years of visual novel fandom, personal anecdotes and features on random things that writers or readers like will fill in the gaps between the large reviews, analyses, and tech breakdowns.
Oh, and sometimes an article is somehow too big to fit in a magazine and ends up on this website instead.
Other times, we'll end up developing a game engine or repairing an old game that no longer works.
Wherever the wind blows, if it takes us somewhere retro, we go.


About Our Mascot

"They say that, in this city...
the ancient one is waiting.
How many years has she lived?"


In a bustling city, the exhausted and overworked masses have become the subject of an urban legend.
On a dreary walk home, on a darkened night, an unfamiliar street lit bright manifests to spirit the lost and tired souls away.
They awake to wooden floorboards, surrounded by shelves of novels and the fizz of CRT screens. Look a little closer and you'd learn that every book is a game with a mundane name.
Inside this library of visual novels, a young girl awaits, with the features of an orange fox, the dress of a maid, and a glint of robotics to the frame of her face.
"Welcome, dear customer, to this retro dream."
The smell of fresh coffee, the sweep of a broomstick, the hum of a fox.
A place no living soul may reach.

In a library of every life that's met its end,
the ancient one is waiting.

How many lives has she read?


Sepia-tan is a youthful girl with boundless energy. She's a bit snarky, a bit smug, and a little bit prideful.
Yet when a customer sits down to play a game—though they'd never turn to see—the onlooking Sepia sits silent and serene.
Her bittersweet expression and the distance in her eyes are things that only come with age and time.

To her customers, yes, she's a youthful girl with boundless energy. Snarky, smug, and filled with pride...
But behind it all lay a lady, one ever so tired and ever so wise.
